MYSORE PAK

Mysore Pak is a popular South Indian sweet originating from the city of Mysore in Karnataka, India. It is a rich and decadent dessert made from gram flour (besan), ghee (clarified butter), sugar, and water. The combination of these ingredients creates a soft, melt-in-the-mouth texture that is both sweet and slightly grainy. Mysore Pak is often flavored with cardamom and garnished with nuts like almonds or cashews. It is a staple during festivals, celebrations, and special occasions in South India.

CHIKEN 65

Chicken 65 is a popular Indian chicken dish that originates from Tamil Nadu. It's known for its spicy, tangy, and deep-fried flavor. The dish consists of marinated chicken pieces coated with a mixture of red chili powder, yogurt, garlic, ginger, and a variety of spices. The marinated chicken is then deep-fried until crispy. It is often garnished with curry leaves, coriander leaves, and sometimes served with a lemon wedge to add a refreshing touch. Chicken 65 is commonly enjoyed as an appetizer or snack at parties, restaurants, and celebrations.
