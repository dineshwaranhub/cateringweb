MORNING

KESARI

Kesari is a popular Indian sweet originating from Tamil Nadu, known for its soft, moist texture and rich flavors. It's made primarily from semolina (rava), sugar, ghee (clarified butter), and flavored with cardamom. Sometimes, Kesari is garnished with nuts like cashews, almonds, and raisins for added texture and flavor. It's a common sweet dish at festivals, celebrations, and special occasions in Tamil Nadu.

uluntha vadai

Uluntha Vadai is a traditional South Indian deep-fried lentil snack. It is made primarily from urad dal (split black gram) and spices such as green chilies, ginger, and curry leaves. The mixture is shaped into small, round patties and deep-fried until golden brown and crispy. Uluntha Vadai is often served with coconut chutney or sambar as a side dish, especially during breakfast or as a tea-time snack in Tamil Nadu.

IDLI

Idli is a traditional South Indian dish made from fermented rice and urad dal (black lentils). It consists of soft, steamed, round cakes that are often served with chutneys and sambar, a spicy lentil soup. Idli is a popular breakfast and snack item in Tamil Nadu and other South Indian states. It is light, nutritious, and easy to digest, making it a favorite among many.

SAMBAR

Sambar is a popular South Indian lentil-based stew that originates from Tamil Nadu. It is ma de with lentils (usually pigeon peas), a variety of vegetables (such as carrots, potatoes, tomatoes, and drumsticks), and flavored with spices including mustard seeds, curry leaves, tamarind, and sambar powder. Sambar is a staple dish in Tamil Nadu and is typically served as a side dish with rice, idli, dosa, or vadai. Its tangy, savory flavor and hearty texture make it a beloved comfort food.

THENGAI CHUTNEY

Thengai Chutney (also known as Coconut Chutney) is a popular South Indian condiment made from grated coconut, green chilies, ginger, and sometimes tamarind or yogurt. It is typically blended together to form a smooth paste and seasoned with mustard seeds, curry leaves, and sometimes urad dal (black gram) for added texture and flavor. Thengai Chutney is commonly s erved as a side dish with dishes like idli, dosa, vadai, and uttapam in Tamil Nadu and other South Indian states.

MINT (PUTHINA CHUTNEY)

Mint or Puthina Chutney is a flavorful South Indian chutney made from fresh mint leaves (puppukaya or puthina), along with green chilies, ginger, and sometimes coconut. The ingredients are blended to form a vibrant, tangy, and mildly spicy chutney that adds a refreshing flavor to meals. It is commonly served with dosas, idlis, vadais, and even snacks like samosas or cutlets in Tamil Nadu. The mint leaves provide a cooling effect and enhance the taste of the dish.

TOMATO CHUTNEY

Tomato Chutney is a popular South Indian condiment made from ripe tomatoes, along with onions, green chilies, and a variety of spices. It is typically tangy, spicy, and slightly sweet in flavor. The ingredients are cooked until the tomatoes are soft and then blended or ground t o form a smooth paste. Tomato chutney is commonly served as a side dish with dosas, idlis, vadais, or even as a spread for sandwiches in Tamil Nadu and other South Indian states.

UTTAPAM

Uttapam is a savory Indian pancake or thick crepe originating from Tamil Nadu. It is made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram), similar to that used for dosas. Uttapam is thicker than dosa and is often topped with ingredients such as chopped onions, tomatoes, green chilies, and coriander leaves before cooking. The toppings are mixed into the batter and fried on a hot griddle until golden brown. Uttapam is commonly served with coconut chutney and sambar as side dishes.

UPPMA

Upma is a traditional South Indian dish made from roasted semolina (rava) cooked with vegetable s such as onions, tomatoes, peas, and carrots, along with spices like mustard seeds, curry le aves, and green chilies. It has a slightly coarse texture and a savory, mild flavor. Upma is a popular breakfast dish in Tamil Nadu and is often enjoyed with coconut chutney or a side of sambar. It's quick to prepare, nutritious, and a favorite choice for a light meal.

VENPONGAL

Ven Pongal is a traditional South Indian dish made from rice and yellow split dal (moong dal) cooked together until they form a creamy consistency. It is flavored with black pepper, cumin seeds, ginger, and sometimes cashews, which add a crunchy texture and nutty flavor. Ven Pongal is often seasoned with ghee (clarified butter) and served hot as a breakfast dish or as an offering during religious rituals in Tamil Nadu and other South Indian states. It is typically accompanied by coconut chutney and sambar.

MUSHROOM BIRIYANI

Mushroom Biryani is a flavorful Indian rice dish made with basmati rice and mushrooms, cooked together with aromatic spices. It combines the earthy flavors of mushrooms with the rich, fragrant spices such as cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and bay leaves. The dish can include other ingredients like yogurt, vegetables, and sometimes cashews or raisins for added texture and flavor. Mushroom Biryani is a popular vegetarian option and is enjoyed in Tamil Nadu and across India. It is typically served with raita (a yogurt-based side dish) or a side of chutney.

POORI AND MASAL

Poori and Masal is a popular South Indian breakfast combination made up of deep-fried Indian bread (poori) and a spiced potato masala (masal) curry. Poori is a type of fried bread made from unleavened wheat flour dough. It puffs up when fried and is golden brown and crispy on the outside. Masal is a spicy potato curry made from boiled and mashed potatoes mixed with spices like mustard seeds, turmeric, cumin, green chilies, and curry leaves. It is often served with poori, and the combination makes for a hearty and satisfying meal. This dish is commonly enjoyed in Tamil Nadu, especially for breakfast or as a snack.

AFTERNOON

BRED HALWA

Bread Halwa is a popular Indian dessert made from bread slices, milk, sugar, ghee (clarified butter), and flavored with cardamom. It's a simple yet delicious dessert that can be made quickly using leftover bread. The bread slices are toasted in ghee until golden, then cooked with milk, sugar, and cardamom until it forms a thick, pudding-like consistency. Sometimes, dried fruits like cashews, raisins, or almonds are added for extra flavor and texture. Bread Halwa is a common treat in Tamil Nadu and other parts of India, often served at celebrations, festivals, or as an everyday dessert.

PICKLE (OR PICL))

Pickle (or picl) is a traditional Indian condiment made from fruits or vegetables that are preserved in a mixture of spices, oil, vinegar, or brine. It can be spicy, tangy, or sweet, depending on the ingredients used. In Tamil Nadu, pickles are a common accompaniment to rice meals, dosas, idlis, and other South Indian dishes. Some popular types include mango pickle, lime pickle, and mixed vegetable pickle. Each has its unique flavor profile, making them an essential part of the meal.

APPALAM

Appalam (also known as Papadam) is a popular snack and side dish in Tamil Nadu and across South India. It's a thin, crispy wafer made from black gram flour (urad dal ) mixed with spices like cumin, black pepper, and sometimes curry leaves. Appalam is typically deep-fried until golden and crispy, but it can also be roasted for a lighter option. It's commonly served with meals, especially d uring lunch or dinner, as an accompaniment to rice dishes like sambar and rasam, or as a snack with chutneys and dips.

VERIERY RICE

A variety rice dish is a popular feature of South Indian cuisine, where rice is often combined with a mix of vegetables, lentils, and spices to create a complete and flavorful meal. Some popular South Indian variety rice dishes include:
Pulao/Pilaf:
A fragrant rice dish made with spices, vegetables, and sometimes meat or seafood. Common varieties include Vegetable Pulao, Peas Pulao, and Biryani.
Curd Rice (Thayir Sadam):
A soothing rice dish made with cooled rice mixed with yogurt, spices, and sometimes tempered with mustard seeds, curry leaves, and green chilies. Lemon Rice (Nimmakaya Annam): Tangy rice flavored with lemon juice, turmeric, and spices, along with peanuts, curry leaves, and sometimes green chilies.
Tamaraind Rice (Pulikachal):
Rice mixed with tamarind pulp, jaggery, and a variety of spices, offering a tangy and spicy flavor.
Coconut Rice:
Rice cooked with grated coconut and flavored with green chilies, curry leaves, and sometimes tempered with mustard seeds and urad dal.
Mixed Vegetable Rice:
Rice mixed with a variety of vegetables and spices, making it a wholesome and colorful meal.
Sambar Rice:
Rice cooked with lentils and flavored with tamarind and spices like mustard seeds, curry leaves, and sambar powder.
Methi Rice (Fenugreek Rice):
Rice mixed with fenugreek leaves and flavored with spices, giving it a slightly bitter but aromatic taste.

WHITE RICE

White rice is a staple food in Tamil Nadu and other parts of South India. It is typically made from milled rice grains that have had their husks, bran, and germ removed, resulting in a white, polished grain. White rice is commonly used in a variety of dishes and is a key component of South Indian meals.

SAMBAR

Sambar is a popular South Indian dish made primarily from lentils, vegetables, tamarind, and a blend of spices. It's a staple in Tamil Nadu cuisine and is typically served with rice, idli, dosa, or vadai as part of a traditional South Indian meal.

Rasam is a popular South Indian soup-like dish known for its tangy, spicy, and flavorful profile. It's often enjoyed as a part of a traditional Tamil Nadu meal, served as an appetizer or a side dish to rice.

Sundai Kai Vatha Kulambu is a traditional Tamil Nadu dish made from dried vegetables (usually sundried drumsticks or okra) cooked in a tangy tamarind-based gravy called vatha kulambu.
